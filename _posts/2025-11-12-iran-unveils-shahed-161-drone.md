In​‍​‌‍​‍‌​‍​‌‍​‍‌ a major move to improve its local drone capabilities, Iran has formally introduced the Shahed-161, a small tactical drone equipped with intelligence and strike capabilities. The disclosure fits with Tehran’s sustained effort to refurbish its drone fleet powered by escalated drone confrontations in the region and generally by the drone warfare trend in the world.

Iranian defense officials inform that the Shahid-161 drone can travel up to 150 kilometers and remain airborne for up to two hours. Besides, its operational ceiling can be 8,000 m (8 km) thus it can carry out missions across various terrains and weather.

The drone looks like a device for surveillance, investigations, and targeted attacks combining the drone with combat flexibility. Though the payload is not specified, the body of the aircraft and the flight duration suggest that it can be loaded with small bombs or a camera system for target identification.

Several military experts consider that the Shahed-161 is a program to consolidate multi-role UAV integration as part of the Iranian Armed Forces, which resulted from studying the conflicts in the region where UAVs were progressively more decisive.

By presenting this model, Iran keeps on communicating that it wants to be independent in technology of defense and is moving forward in the race to be the Middle East’s top drone ​‍​‌‍​‍‌​‍​‌‍​‍‌developer.
